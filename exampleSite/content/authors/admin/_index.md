I am a Research Scientist at the West African Centre for Cell Biology of Infectious Pathogens (WACCBIP), University of Ghana. My research is centered around combating malaria drug resistance at the intersection of molecular surveillance and computational modeling.

I develop bioinformatics pipelines and apply population genetics methods to track the emergence and spread of drug-resistant *Plasmodium falciparum* across Ghana and West Africa. My work directly informs national malaria control policy through evidence-based insights delivered to the National Malaria Elimination Program.

I hold an MSc in Computer Science and a BSc in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Ghana.
